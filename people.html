Van der Stocken Tom

Tom Van der Stocken is a professor of Ecology at VUB since 2022 and a research affiliate at NASA-Caltech's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) since 2014. His research focuses on tropical wetland ecology, global change biology, and coastal and ocean studies, using methods like numerical modeling, remote sensing, and citizen science. He explores how coastal wetlands respond to global change and their role in the climate system, collaborating internationally with NASA-Caltech, MLML, and UCLA.

He was a Marie Skłodowska-Curie fellow (2020-2022) and previously worked as a postdoctoral scholar at Caltech and JPL. Van der Stocken contributed to the development of global coastal maps and the ECCO project. He earned a PhD in Physical Geography, focusing on mangrove forest ecology, and won the 2020 Ignace Vanderschueren PhD Prize.
